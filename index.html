Understanding the I-IV-V Progression

The I, IV, and V chords are the backbone of countless hit songs. They carry the emotional weight and tonal structure of most music across genres.

The I (root) chord is your musical home. Add the second (II) for more depth and character.

The IV chord adds richness, especially when played as a 7th chord. It introduces soulful and jazzy textures.

The V chord creates tension, pushing your music back to the I chord. Keep it simple—its power lies in its resolution.

Practice makes perfect. Every great player started with these chords.

Whether you’re producing electronic beats, performing live, or learning your first song—these chords will always serve you well.
